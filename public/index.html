The current state of home automation

Lots of options

Expensive!

Standards

  • X10
  • Insteon
  • Z-Wave
  • Zigbee
  • Wifi
  • Rube Goldberg machines

X10

  • Developed in 1975
  • Most widely available
  • Primarily uses power line wiring for signals
  • One command at a time, relatively slow, limited

Insteon

  • Roughly around since the mid 90s
  • Available in most stores
  • Fairly expensive
  • Mesh network

Z-Wave

  • Not as widely available locally, but lots of products online
  • Mesh network
  • Getting cheaper
  • Low power RF

Zigbee

  • Standardized in 2003, IEEE 802.15
  • Very low power RF, secure
  • Mesh network

Wifi

  • If I have to explain what wifi is...
  • Not quite the same as the others, however much more ubiquitous
  • Some popular devices use Wifi (Nest, Phillips HUE, Sonos - sort of)

And the winner is...

Z-Wave

Side note: the real winner...

The real "winner" in the automation space will be a device that speaks all of these protocols.

Why Z-Wave

  • Mesh network
  • Wireless
  • Lightweight
  • "easy" to work with - Razberry

Available Z-Wave Devices

  • Thermostats
  • Light switches
  • Wall outlets
  • Motorized blinds
  • Door locks
  • Irrigation control
  • Likely more...

Infrastructure

  • Hub / Controller - receives communication via HTTP, speaks Z-Wave
  • Devices - receive Z-Wave signals

NFC

Near field communication

Has been around for a while

Powered & non-powered

How NFC Works

  • Antenna produces electric or magnetic field
  • The receiver must be within the field
  • Range is incredibly small, hard to eavesdrop

Quick NFC Demo

Why not Bluetooth or RFID?

We'll be talking bluetooth later, hold your horses...

No reason, but all three solve different types of problems

  • NFC is very cheap by comparison
  • NFC is more secure do to range restrictions
  • However NFC is very limited by its data transfer capability

Common NFC Use Cases

  • Payments / Commerce
  • Ticketing / Coupons
  • Identity
  • Bootstrapping more complex connections - Android Beam

You may have already seen it or be using it

Some cities use NFC for their transportation systems
